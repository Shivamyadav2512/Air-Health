Why should we care about Air pollution?

The rate at which urban air pollution has grown across India is alarming. A vast majority of cities are caught in the toxic web as air quality fails to meet health-based standards. Almost all cities are reeling under severe particulate pollution while newer pollutants like oxides of nitrogen and air toxics have begun to add to the public health challenge.

Effects of Air pollution

High air pollution levels can cause immediate health problems including: Aggravated cardiovascular and respiratory illness Added stress to heart and lungs, which must work harder to supply the body with oxygen. Long-term exposure to polluted air can have permanent health effects such as: Accelerated aging of the lungs Loss of lung capacity and decreased lung function
